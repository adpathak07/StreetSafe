Why This Matters

Cities with higher foreign-born populations and lower incomes are disproportionately harmed by infrastructure failures — residents are less able to absorb costs of vehicle damage, healthcare from injuries, or reduced mobility. This tool helps cities prioritize repairs where the human cost of inaction is highest.

⚠️ Data Limitations

Geographic Coverage
Cyvl data covers Beverly, MA only (168 miles of roads). Results cannot be generalized to other cities without additional Cyvl scans.
Census Data Lag
ACS demographic data is from 2023 5-year estimates. Population and income figures may not reflect current conditions.
Risk Score Simplification
The IEPS risk score uses PCI label + sidewalk condition. It does not account for traffic volume, pedestrian density, or seasonal factors.
Asset Condition Matching
Sidewalk conditions are matched to road segments by proximity — not a direct 1:1 mapping. Some segments show "None" where no nearby asset data exists.

✅ Ethics & Responsibility

No Personal Data
Zero personally identifiable information used. All demographic data is aggregated at city level from public Census sources.
Transparent Scoring
Risk formula is fully disclosed: Risk = f(PCI label) + asset condition penalty. No black-box algorithms.
Human-in-the-Loop
This tool supports city planners — it does not replace engineering assessments. All outputs are labeled as decision-support, not final recommendations.
Open License
